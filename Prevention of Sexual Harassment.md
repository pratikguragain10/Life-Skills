# Prevention of Sexual Harassment

## Question 1: What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?

**Physical Behaviour:**
- Unwanted touching, hugging, or physical contact.
- Invasion of personal space.
- Physical intimidation.

**Verbal Behaviour:**
- Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favours.
- Sexual comments, jokes, or innuendo.
- Offensive remarks about appearance or body.
- Persistent requests for dates despite refusal.

**Non-verbal/Visual Behaviour:**
- Displaying sexually explicit materials.
- Sexual gestures or suggestive looks.
- Forwarding inappropriate emails or messages.
- Staring or ogling.

**Quid Pro Quo Harassment:**
- Implying employment benefits in exchange for sexual favours.
- Using position of power for sexual coercion.
- Making promotion/evaluation dependent on sexual compliance.

**Online/Digital Harassment:**
- Sending inappropriate messages via email, chat, or social media.
- Sharing intimate photos without consent.
- Cyberstalking or persistent unwanted digital contact.

**Hostile Work Environment:**
- Creating an atmosphere that makes people feel unsafe.
- Excluding someone based on gender.
- Retaliation against those who reject advances.

## Question 2: What would you do in case you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?

**If You Face Harassment:**

**Immediate Steps:**
- Tell the person to stop (if safe to do so).
- Document everything: dates, times, witnesses, exact words/actions.
- Save any evidence: messages, emails, photos.

**Reporting Process:**
- Submit a written complaint to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).
- The ICC must acknowledge your complaint within specified timelines.
- You have the right to have a representative during proceedings.

**During Investigation:**
- Cooperate fully with the inquiry process.
- All matters will be kept confidential.
- You are protected against retaliation.
- Interim relief measures can be requested (transfer, leave, etc.).

**After Investigation:**
- The ICC will provide recommendations.
- The employer must implement appropriate disciplinary action.
- You have the right to appeal any decision.

**If You Witness Harassment:**

**Immediate Response:**
- Ensure the victim's safety first.
- Intervene if safe to do so (without escalating the situation).
- Offer support to the victim privately.

**Reporting:**
- Report the incident to HR or ICC.
- Provide a witness statement if required.
- Maintain confidentiality about the matter.

**Support Actions:**
- Encourage the victim to report if they haven't already.
- Offer to accompany them to meetings with HR/ICC.
- Avoid gossip or discussing the incident with others.
- Continue to be supportive without being intrusive.
